# 3D Reconstruction Methods Comparison

## 1. COLMAP (Photogrammetry)

### Input
- Multiple overlapping images

### Output
- Sparse and dense 3D point clouds

### Advantages
- Open source
- Accurate reconstruction

### Challenges
- Requires many photos

### PMW Use Case
Can reconstruct historical sites from photographs.


---

## 2. NeRF (Neural Radiance Fields)

### Input
- Multiple images and camera positions

### Output
- High-quality novel views of scenes

### Advantages
- Very realistic rendering

### Challenges
- High GPU requirements

### PMW Use Case
Can generate immersive visualizations of heritage sites.


---

## 3. Gaussian Splatting

### Input
- Multi-view images

### Output
- Real-time 3D scene rendering

### Advantages
- Faster than NeRF
- High visual quality

### Challenges
- Newer technology

### PMW Use Case
Suitable for interactive visualization of reconstructed locations.


## Conclusion

For beginners, COLMAP is the easiest starting point.
Gaussian Splatting offers faster rendering, while NeRF produces highly realistic results.
